At Saud International School, the Middle School Stage (Grades 6โ8) marks a critical transition in a student's educational journey โ a time when foundational knowledge is deepened, independence is nurtured, and future pathways begin to take shape.
Our curriculum for Grades 6 to 8 is thoughtfully designed to provide academic rigor, emotional development, and structured subject specialization. We prepare students not just for high school, but for a life of critical thinking, responsibility, and intellectual engagement.

๐ Curriculum Framework Overview

Middle School students follow a curriculum based on the British education system, gradually preparing them for IGCSE studies in Grades 9 and 10. Our program aligns with internationally benchmarked standards while incorporating key educational requirements mandated by the Ministry of Education in Saudi Arabia.

The framework includes:

  • Core academic subjects taught by specialist subject teachers

  • Skills-based learning in analysis, writing, problem-solving, and communication

  • Continuous mapping of curriculum content for smooth progression

  • Integration of digital learning, inquiry, and interdisciplinary themes

Unlike in Elementary where homeroom teachers deliver most instruction, Middle School students are taught by subject specialists trained in the academic content, assessment, and pedagogy of their particular disciplines. This allows for:

  • Deeper content knowledge

  • Advanced instructional strategies

  • Focused academic support in subject-specific areas

  • Structured and diverse academic routine

Students rotate between classes throughout the day, experiencing a more structured and diverse academic routine โ similar to the high school model, ensuring a gradual and confident transition into senior school.

๐ Academic Progression & Curriculum Mapping

Our curriculum is vertically aligned, meaning each grade level builds upon the previous year's knowledge and skill set. This ensures students:

  • Develop confidence in tackling more abstract and analytical content

  • Strengthen foundational skills in core subjects

  • Receive seamless academic progression toward IGCSE-level coursework

  • Benefit from synchronized curriculum mapping across subjects

Our teachers collaborate to ensure that curriculum mapping, learning objectives, and assessment rubrics are synchronized across subjects and grade levels.

๐ง Focus on Cognitive and Emotional Growth

Middle School is not just about academics. It is a time of personal transformation. Our approach supports:

  • Critical thinking and independent learning

  • Time management and study skills

  • Emotional regulation and social development

  • Responsible technology use and digital citizenship

Students are supported through a blend of academic mentoring, classroom activities, assemblies, and values education โ equipping them to grow in confidence, purpose, and self-awareness.

๐ Assessments & Checkpoint Exams (Grade 8)

Assessment in Middle School is both formative and summative:

  • Regular quizzes, assignments, projects, and presentations

  • Mid-year and end-of-year exams

  • Continuous academic tracking and teacher feedback

  • Cambridge Lower Secondary Checkpoint Exams in Grade 8

In Grade 8, students are prepared for the Cambridge Lower Secondary Checkpoint Exams (or equivalent international benchmark), which assess progress in English, Mathematics, and Science. These exams help guide subject selection in high school and provide parents and students with an international benchmark of readiness for IGCSE studies.

  • Students and teachers

  • Parents and the academic team

  • Teachers across grade levels for consistent student support

  • Individualized strategies for improvement

Each student's learning profile is carefully tracked year by year to ensure that strengths are enhanced, gaps are addressed, and students are given individualized strategies for improvement. This system ensures that by the time a student enters Grade 9, they are academically prepared and emotionally mature to handle the demands of the IGCSE program.
